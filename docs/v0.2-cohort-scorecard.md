# LinGet v0.2 prerelease cohort scorecard

LinGet v0.2 stays a prerelease until a ten-person Linux cohort demonstrates that the terminal-first maintenance loop is useful and safe. Downloads and repository clones are discovery signals, not proof of active use.

## Promotion gates

All gates must pass before v0.2 is promoted from prerelease to a stable release:

| Gate | Pass threshold | Evidence |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| First-session activation | At least 8 of 10 participants reach Today after provider detection | Participant check-in |
| Plan comprehension | At least 8 of 10 can correctly describe what a reviewed plan will change | Short post-task question |
| Verified operation | At least 7 of 10 complete one install, update, or removal with a verification receipt | Redacted receipt outcome |
| Safety | 0 unreviewed mutations and 0 unexplained package changes | Participant report plus receipt |
| Task success | At least 8 of 10 complete their chosen maintenance task without facilitator intervention | Observation or participant report |
| Week-one retention | At least 4 of 10 use LinGet again within seven days | Day-seven check-in |
| Usefulness | At least 7 of 10 rate LinGet 4 or 5 on a five-point usefulness scale | Day-seven check-in |

A failed safety gate blocks promotion regardless of the aggregate score. A failed non-safety gate creates a Beads issue with the observed problem, affected provider, and reproduction steps before another cohort run.

## Test script

1. Install the signed/checksummed prerelease artifact on a non-critical Linux machine or disposable VM.
2. Launch `linget` and let provider detection finish.
3. Ask the participant what Today recommends and why.
4. Choose one APT, Flatpak, or npm operation that the participant already intended to perform.
5. Review the provider, exact command, expected changes, privilege requirement, and plan fidelity before confirming.
6. Complete the operation and inspect the verification receipt.
7. Run `linget cohort-report --output cohort-report.json`, review the generated
   summary, and share it with the facilitator only if the participant agrees.
8. Ask: "What changed? Did anything surprise you? How useful was this from 1 to 5?"
9. Repeat the usage/usefulness check seven days later.

Participants must not be pressured to mutate a system merely to satisfy the test. A read-only walkthrough can validate comprehension but does not count toward the verified-operation gate.

The cohort report is local-only and aggregate. It is supporting evidence, not
telemetry and not proof of plan comprehension, task success, retention, or
usefulness. See [the report privacy contract](cohort-report.md).
