# Privacy-safe sharing loop

LinGet is a personal utility, not a collaborative network. Its natural loop is a small referral loop triggered only after demonstrated usefulness:

`safe useful outcome → optional release-link share → referred first session → activation → later optional share`

## Eligibility and prompt

Offer the prompt at day seven only when the participant rates usefulness 4 or 5 and reports no safety concern. Never make sharing a condition of support, participation, or stable promotion.

> If LinGet made a real maintenance task clearer, you may share this public guide with one Linux user who would benefit. No tracking link or referral code is used, and there is no reward. Please do not share anyone’s system details: https://github.com/Eslamasabry/LinGet/blob/master/docs/cohort-v0.2/participant-guide.md

The participant chooses whether, where, and with whom to share. The project does not ask for the recipient, contact list, screenshot, proof, or message contents.

## Aggregate measurement

Measure once per seven-day cycle:

- eligible participants;
- participants offered the prompt;
- participants who say they chose to share;
- new first sessions that self-classify discovery as “shared by another user”;
- referred first sessions that reach Today.

The report calculates offer-to-share rate, referred activation rate, and referred activations per cohort participant. These are directional because they are self-reported and deliberately use no tracking links. Do not optimize this loop until activation, safety, and usefulness gates pass; growth cannot compensate for an unsafe product.

## Loop guardrails

- One optional prompt per participant, only at day seven.
- No incentives, address books, automated messages, unique links, cookies, or identity graph.
- No public testimonials or quoted feedback from cohort responses.
- No claims about adoption based on clones or downloads alone.
- Pause the loop immediately after a safety report.
